## The Three Credential Options Worth Considering

### Option 1: CQF (Certificate in Quantitative Finance)
**The fast credential.**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Duration | 6 months part-time |
| Cost | ~$15,000 SGD |
| Format | Online + in-person events |
| Reputation | Well-known in quant industry. Accepted as "this person took the initiative to learn quant finance properly." |
| What you learn | Stochastic calculus, derivatives pricing, numerical methods, risk, ML for finance |
| Who recognizes it | Most quant funds, especially in Asia. Paul Wilmott (founder) is a respected name. |
| ROI for you | **HIGH.** Fast, focused, directly applicable. Won't distract from building/interviewing. |

**Verdict: Strongly consider.** It's the highest ROI credential for your situation. It won't replace a PhD, but it signals "I'm serious about quant finance" and covers the mathematical foundations (stochastic calculus, derivatives pricing) that your ML background lacks.

**Website**: cqf.com

### Option 2: NUS MFE (Master of Financial Engineering)
**The Singapore network play.**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Duration | 1 year full-time OR 2 years part-time |
| Cost | ~$58,000 SGD (2025 rates) |
| Format | On-campus, NUS Kent Ridge |
| Reputation | Top MFE in Asia. Strong placement at GIC, Temasek, DBS, local hedge funds. |
| What you learn | Stochastic calculus, derivatives, portfolio optimization, risk management, ML for finance, capstone project |
| Who recognizes it | Every financial institution in Singapore. Strong alumni network in SG finance. |
| EP boost | NUS degree helps with COMPASS framework for EP eligibility |
| ROI for you | **MEDIUM-HIGH.** Excellent network + credential, but 1-2 years is a long time. |

**Verdict: Consider the part-time option IF you can work simultaneously.** The NUS brand in Singapore is unmatched for local finance roles. The alumni network will get your resume past HR filters at GIC, Temasek, and all Singapore-based funds.

**Key consideration**: Part-time MFE means 2 years. During those 2 years, you could also be working (with LOC) and building your track record. The degree + work experience combination is powerful.

**Website**: mfe.nus.edu.sg

**Similar alternatives:**
- NUS MSc Quantitative Finance (more theory, less engineering)
- NTU MSc Financial Engineering (cheaper, still respected)
- SMU MSc Applied Finance (more practical, shorter)

## Singapore-Specific Considerations

### Employment Pass (EP) COMPASS Framework

Singapore uses a points-based framework (COMPASS) for EP applications. Relevant factors:

| Factor | How Education Helps |
|--------|-------------------|
| Qualifications | Degree from top institution = more points. NUS MFE would count. CQF probably won't (it's a certificate, not a degree). |
| Salary | At $500K+, you'll easily pass the salary threshold regardless of education |
| Company support | Large funds (Citadel, Point72) have no trouble getting EPs approved |
| Skills bonus | AI/ML is on the shortage occupation list — this helps regardless |

**Bottom line**: For the roles you're targeting ($500K+), the employer will handle EP sponsorship and the salary alone exceeds the threshold. Education helps on COMPASS points but isn't the bottleneck.

## What Actually Gets You Hired at $1M

In order of importance:

1. **Demonstrated ability to generate alpha** (the Alpha Lab + paper trading track record)
2. **Financial markets fluency** (can you discuss factors, risk, portfolio construction with a PM?)
3. **Technical depth** (ML + stats + quant methods)
4. **Credential signal** (PhD > MFE > CQF > nothing — but each level matters less than the ones above)
5. **Network** (someone vouches for you)
6. **Cultural fit** (can you partner with PMs, build a team, communicate with C-suite?)

A credential helps with #4 and #5. But #1, #2, and #3 are what get you through the interview.

**The credential doesn't get you hired. It gets you through the door. What you know gets you hired.**
